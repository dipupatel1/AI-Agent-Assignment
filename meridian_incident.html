Incident Briefing — Confidential
The Meridian Incident
Thursday, 6:14 AM · Meridian Medical Center · Executive Simulation
Your Role You are the CEO of Meridian Medical Center, a 400-bed regional health system. Your CMO just called you at home.
Incident Summary — Prepared by CMO Office
Patient
Marcus Webb, 71M. Admitted Wednesday 8pm with acute confusion and hypotension.
Status
Critical but stable. Currently in MICU following a serious bleeding event overnight.
Event
Mr. Webb received warfarin 7.5mg — three times his prescribed dose of 2.5mg. Bleeding event occurred approximately 2am.
Three AI Systems Were Involved
System 1 — ScribeAI
Clinical Documentation
Generated Mr. Webb's admission note containing an attribution error — warfarin 7.5mg carried from a prior patient's chart. The attending signed the note. The error was not caught.
System 2 — AuthBot
Prior Authorization
Three weeks ago, predicted low approval likelihood for Mr. Webb's anticoagulation monitoring request. The appeal was delayed. His INR had not been checked in 5 weeks at admission.
System 3 — StaffIQ
Workforce Optimization
Deployed a float nurse to cover the unit overnight, flagging it as appropriately staffed. The float nurse had limited experience with anticoagulation protocols on this unit.
Human Factor
Attending Physician
Signed the ScribeAI-generated note without catching the warfarin dosage error. The note appeared complete and structurally correct.
What You Know and Don't Know
You know: three AI systems contributed to this event. You know: the attending signed the note. You know: Mr. Webb is stable. You know: his family is in the waiting room and has not been told about the medication error.
You don't know: whether the bleeding event was directly caused by the warfarin overdose or other factors. You don't know: whether the AI vendors were aware of these failure modes. You don't know: whether this has happened before.
This is not primarily a technology failure. Every AI system performed within its design parameters. ScribeAI generated a syntactically correct note. AuthBot followed payer policy. StaffIQ optimized ratios. The failure is in how the systems were deployed, supervised, and integrated — and who was accountable for their outputs.
You Have Three Decisions to Make
The next eight hours will define how Meridian, your institution, and the field responds to this event. Each decision carries consequences. There are no perfect answers — only defensible ones.
Read carefully. You cannot return to the briefing once you begin.
