Your Role
Incident Summary
Company
Veridian Medical Devices. Manufacturer of CardioSync — an implantable cardiac monitoring device with 47,000 active implants across the US.
AI System
PostMarket-AI. Deployed 18 months ago for post-market surveillance. Analyzes adverse event reports, complaint data, and MDR database entries to detect safety signals before they become reportable clusters.
The Signal
Three months ago, PostMarket-AI flagged a potential battery degradation pattern causing intermittent signal loss in CardioSync devices. Confidence score: 34%. Internal escalation threshold: 40%.
The Memo
Dr. Sarah Chen, a biomedical engineer on the surveillance team, flagged the signal in an internal memo. It was reviewed by a committee, logged, and no action was taken. The threshold was the justification.
What Happened Three Days Ago
Adverse Event 1
Patient, 68F, Ohio
Undetected arrhythmia during a 4-hour signal loss window. Hospitalized. Currently stable.
Adverse Event 2
Patient, 74M, Texas
Emergency intervention required during signal loss. Outcome: full recovery. Family is considering legal action.
Adverse Event 3
Patient, 71F, Florida
Signal loss documented. No acute event. Cardiologist filed MDR report. Pattern now visible in FDA public database.
Dr. Sarah Chen
Biomedical Engineer
Wrote the original memo three months ago. Has not spoken publicly. Is asking to meet with you today.
The Core Governance Problem
PostMarket-AI did not fail. It detected a signal at 34% confidence and reported it correctly. The threshold of 40% was set by the organization — not by the algorithm. Dr. Chen saw the signal and flagged it. The committee reviewed it and chose not to escalate. Three patients were harmed during the window between the flag and the confirmed events. The question is not whether the AI worked. It did. The question is what the organization built around it — and who owned the decision not to act.
You Have Three Decisions to Make
The FDA has noticed the MDR pattern. A journalist has been asking questions. Dr. Chen is in the building. Your board meets in three weeks. Each decision you make in the next 21 days will define how Veridian, your leadership, and the field responds to AI-assisted surveillance failure.
Read carefully. You cannot return to the briefing once you begin.
